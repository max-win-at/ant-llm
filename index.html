How it works

  • Habitat is the network address space — a graph of URLs (nodes) and latencies (edges).
  • Movement is the act of fetch() — each request is physical locomotion through the network.
  • Distance is measured in RTT (round-trip time) — slow APIs are swamps, fast APIs are highways.
  • Pheromones are temporal data points on URL nodes, decaying via e-λ·Δt.
  • Food is the JSON payload — flat structures are sugar (quick energy), nested objects are protein (brood generation).
  • Predators are HTTP error codes: 429 (Ant-Lion), 5xx (Storm), timeouts (Swamp).
  • Ants are persistent async state machines stored in LocalStorage.
  • Nest stores food as browser Cookies with natural expiry.
